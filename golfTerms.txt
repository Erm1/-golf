Choke: A derogatory term describing poor play that results from nervousness. 
       (Early in his career, some critics claimed Tom Watson choked under pressure).
Birdie: A score of one under par on a hole. 
        (Her birdie on the 10th hole was a turning point in the match).
Bogey: A score of one over par on a hole. (The bogey on 18 cost him the championship).
Dormie: The point in match play when a player is up in a match by the same number of holes that remain. 
        (When Lanny Wadkins had his opponent dormie three, it seemed like the Americans would win the Ryder Cup).
Eagle: A score of two-under-par on a hole. (His eagle on the 17th hole assured his victory.)
